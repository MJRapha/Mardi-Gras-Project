Queen

Costumes during Mardi Gras balls represent Royalty. The most extravagant costumes are mostly worn by kings and queens. The Krewe of Comus essentially were the first krewe to have themed parades.

Masks

In the beginning, masks worn during Mardi Gras allowed wearers to escape society and class constraints. When wearing a mask, carnival goers were free to be whomever they wanted to be, and mingle with whatever class they desired to mingle with.

King Cake

King Cakes are oval-shaped to symbolize the unity of faiths. Each cake is decorated in the traditional Mardi Gras colors - purple representing justice, green representing faith, and gold representing power.

The Mardi Gras Festival
Mardi Gras refers to events of the Carnival celebration, beginning on or after the Christian feasts of the Epiphany and culminating on the day before Ash Wednesday, which is known as Shrove Tuesday.

How long is a Mardi Gras parade?
From one to four hours! A parade can last from one to four hours, so be sure to arrive early. It may also take a little while to get back to your car after the parade. This means you need to be prepared. Here are some of the items you must bring to a Mardi Gras parade: Sturdy bag or two to pick up beads and trinkets, Water and snacks, Rain gear/umbrella, Hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen.
